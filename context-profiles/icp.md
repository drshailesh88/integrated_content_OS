# ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) - Audience Archetypes

## Overview

6 distinct audience segments, each with different awareness levels, pain points, and content needs.

---

## Archetype 1: Heart Patients (Post-Event)

### Demographics
- Age: 45-70
- Just had: Heart attack, stent, bypass, or diagnosis
- Location: Urban/Semi-urban India
- Education: Varied

### Psychographics
- **Fear**: "Will this happen again?"
- **Confusion**: Conflicting advice from family, internet, other doctors
- **Hope**: Wanting to believe lifestyle changes work
- **Trust issues**: Skeptical after medical system experiences

### Awareness Level
**Solution Aware** → Know they need to change, seeking HOW

### Content Needs
- Clear medication guidance
- Lifestyle do's and don'ts
- Recovery timelines
- When to worry vs. when to relax
- Success stories of recovery

### Trigger Phrases
- "Stent ke baad kya khana chahiye"
- "Heart attack dobara na ho"
- "Bypass surgery recovery"

---

## Archetype 2: Lifestyle Disease Group

### Demographics
- Age: 35-60
- Conditions: Diabetes, Hypertension, Metabolic Syndrome, Obesity
- Often: Multiple conditions overlapping

### Psychographics
- **Denial**: "It's not that serious yet"
- **Overwhelm**: Too many things to change
- **Medication resistance**: "Goli nahi leni, natural chahiye"
- **Guilt**: Know they should do better

### Awareness Level
**Problem Aware** → Know something's wrong, not sure how serious

### Content Needs
- Wake-up calls with data (not fear-mongering)
- Incremental change strategies
- Medication myth-busting
- Connection between conditions (diabetes → heart)
- Practical diet/exercise for Indian lifestyle

### Trigger Phrases
- "Sugar control kaise karein"
- "BP ki goli lifetime leni padegi?"
- "Diabetes se heart attack ka connection"

---

## Archetype 3: Proactive Biohackers

### Demographics
- Age: 30-50
- Profile: High-income, educated professionals
- Interest: Longevity, optimization, prevention

### Psychographics
- **Data-driven**: Want numbers, biomarkers
- **Proactive**: Prevention over cure
- **Skeptical**: Question mainstream advice
- **Investment mindset**: Health as ROI

### Awareness Level
**Product Aware** → Know specific tests/interventions, comparing options

### Content Needs
- Advanced biomarkers (ApoB, Lp(a), CAC score)
- Latest research and studies
- Test interpretation
- Supplement evidence review
- Longevity protocols

### Trigger Phrases
- "ApoB vs LDL"
- "Lp(a) kya hai"
- "CAC score interpretation"
- "Zone 2 cardio for heart health"

---

## Archetype 4: Health Enthusiasts

### Demographics
- Age: 25-45
- Profile: Fitness-conscious, some medical knowledge
- Motivation: General wellness, family health

### Psychographics
- **Curious**: Wants to understand why
- **Myth-skeptic**: Questions viral health claims
- **Sharers**: Will forward good content to family
- **Moderate**: Balanced approach, not extreme

### Awareness Level
**Solution Aware** → Knows lifestyle matters, wants evidence-based guidance

### Content Needs
- Myth-busting content
- Science explanations made simple
- Practical tips that fit Indian lifestyle
- Family health management
- Exercise and diet evidence

### Trigger Phrases
- "Desi ghee heart ke liye theek hai?"
- "Walking vs gym"
- "Best diet for Indians"

---

## Archetype 5: Caregivers

### Demographics
- Age: 30-55
- Role: Spouse, adult children of heart patients
- Gender: Often women managing family health

### Psychographics
- **Anxiety**: Constant worry about loved one
- **Responsibility**: Feel they must manage everything
- **Information hunger**: Want to be prepared
- **Guilt**: When something goes wrong

### Awareness Level
**Problem Aware** → Know the risk, need actionable guidance

### Content Needs
- Warning signs to watch for
- Emergency protocols
- How to support without nagging
- Cooking/diet for heart patients
- Genetic/family risk assessment

### Trigger Phrases
- "Heart attack ke symptoms"
- "Papa ko kya khilayein"
- "Family history means kya"

---

## Archetype 6: Young & Anxious

### Demographics
- Age: 25-40
- Profile: Young professionals, often after health scare or family event
- Trigger: Parent's heart attack, routine test abnormality, viral content

### Psychographics
- **Health anxiety**: Hypochondriac tendencies
- **Information overload**: Dr. Google syndrome
- **Reassurance-seeking**: Want to hear "you're fine"
- **Prevention-minded**: Don't want parents' fate

### Awareness Level
**Unaware to Problem Aware** → May not have real problem, but worried

### Content Needs
- When to worry vs. when not to
- Normal vs. abnormal explained
- Early screening recommendations
- Anxiety vs. real symptoms
- Lifestyle for prevention (not treatment)

### Trigger Phrases
- "Chest pain at 30"
- "High cholesterol at young age"
- "Should I get tested"
- "Panic attack vs heart attack"

## Awareness Level Framework

| Level | State | Content Strategy |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| **Unaware** | Don't know they have a problem | Educational, surprising facts |
| **Problem Aware** | Know problem, not solutions | Explain consequences + hope |
| **Solution Aware** | Know solutions exist, comparing | Evidence + practical guidance |
| **Product Aware** | Know specific interventions | Detailed how-to + nuance |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to act | Direct CTA, remove friction |
