# Hook Patterns for Carousel First Slides

High-converting hook patterns for cardiology content.

---

## The Hook Formula

```
[Pattern] + [Specific Topic] + [Curiosity Gap]
```

**Example:**
- Pattern: "X things you didn't know about..."
- Topic: "LDL cholesterol"
- Curiosity Gap: "that could save your life"

**Result:** "5 things you didn't know about LDL cholesterol that could save your life"

---

## Hook Categories

### 1. NUMBER HOOKS
The most reliable pattern for engagement.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| X things about [topic] | 7 things about statins your doctor won't tell you |
| X myths about [topic] | 5 myths about heart attacks that could kill you |
| X reasons why [action] | 3 reasons why your LDL target is wrong |
| X signs of [condition] | 4 signs of heart disease you're ignoring |
| X mistakes with [topic] | 6 mistakes people make with blood pressure |

**Best numbers:** 3, 5, 7, 10 (odd numbers outperform even)

### 2. QUESTION HOOKS
Create curiosity and invite engagement.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| What if [belief] is wrong? | What if everything you know about cholesterol is wrong? |
| Is [common practice] hurting you? | Is your "healthy" diet causing heart disease? |
| Why does [surprising thing] happen? | Why do marathon runners have heart attacks? |
| What [authority] won't tell you | What Big Pharma won't tell you about statins |
| Are you making this [topic] mistake? | Are you making this fatal blood pressure mistake? |

### 3. MYTH-BUSTING HOOKS
Challenge assumptions to create engagement.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| The truth about [topic] | The truth about eggs and heart disease |
| [Common belief] is a lie | "Eat less, move more" is a lie |
| Stop believing [myth] | Stop believing LDL doesn't matter |
| This [topic] myth could kill you | This statin myth could kill you |
| [Authority] got [topic] wrong | The AHA got dietary fat completely wrong |

### 4. FEAR/URGENCY HOOKS
Use sparingly but effectively for high stakes topics.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| [X] could be killing you | Your breakfast could be killing you |
| The silent [condition] | The silent heart attack you're ignoring |
| Warning signs of [condition] | Warning signs your arteries are clogged |
| Before it's too late | What to do before your next heart attack |
| This mistake is deadly | This blood pressure mistake is deadly |

### 5. AUTHORITY HOOKS
Leverage credibility and trust.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| What [expert] wishes you knew | What cardiologists wish you knew about statins |
| [Study] just revealed | The CLEAR trial just revealed something shocking |
| New research shows | New research shows exercise beats medication |
| As a [credential], I must warn you | As a cardiologist, I must warn you about this |
| Inside [institution/trial] | Inside the trial that changed cardiology forever |

### 6. CONTRARIAN HOOKS
Challenge mainstream views (with evidence).

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Why [mainstream advice] is wrong | Why "low-fat" advice was completely wrong |
| [Common practice] doesn't work | Why exercise alone doesn't lower cholesterol |
| The problem with [popular thing] | The problem with popular heart health advice |
| [Unexpected thing] is [opposite] | This "unhealthy" food is actually heart-protective |
| Forget [old advice] | Forget everything you know about saturated fat |

## Hook Formulas by Slide Type

### For TIPS Carousels
- "X tips to [achieve goal]"
- "How to [solve problem] in X steps"
- "X things you should do for [health goal]"

### For MYTH-BUSTING Carousels
- "X [topic] myths busted"
- "Stop believing these [topic] lies"
- "The biggest [topic] myths debunked"

### For DATA Carousels
- "[Study name] just changed everything"
- "What X,000 patients taught us about [topic]"
- "The data on [topic] is clear"

### For STORY Carousels
- "This patient taught me everything about [topic]"
- "The [condition] case that haunts me"
- "What [X] years of practice taught me"

---

## Hook Quality Checklist

### Must Have (at least 2)
- [ ] Number (3, 5, 7, 10)
- [ ] Question mark
- [ ] Specific topic
- [ ] Curiosity gap
- [ ] Authority marker

### Avoid
- Generic phrases ("Check this out")
- Clickbait without substance
- Overpromising ("This will cure...")
- Vague language ("Some things about...")

## Cardiology-Specific Hooks

### Statins
- "5 statin myths that could cost you your life"
- "What I tell every patient who refuses statins"
- "The real side effects of statins (based on data)"

### LDL/Cholesterol
- "Your LDL target is probably wrong"
- "7 things that raise LDL (that aren't food)"
- "Why I stopped worrying about total cholesterol"

### Heart Attack/Prevention
- "4 warning signs your heart is struggling"
- "The test that predicts heart attacks (not cholesterol)"
- "What actually causes heart attacks (it's not what you think)"

### Blood Pressure
- "6 BP mistakes making your hypertension worse"
- "White coat hypertension: When to worry, when to ignore"
- "The blood pressure number that matters most"

### GLP-1s
- "What Ozempic does to your heart (the data)"
- "5 things about GLP-1s your doctor won't mention"
- "Who should NOT take GLP-1 medications"

### CAC Scoring
- "Your CAC score explained in 60 seconds"
- "What a zero CAC score really means"
- "3 reasons CAC changed how I practice cardiology"

## A/B Testing Framework

### Test These Variables
1. Number vs no number
2. Question vs statement
3. Fear-based vs curiosity-based
4. Specific vs broad
5. Authority-led vs data-led

## Anti-Patterns for Hooks

### Weak Hooks
- "Important information about [topic]"
- "Things to know about [topic]"
- "Here's some advice about [topic]"
- "Learn about [topic]"

### Overused Hooks
- "You won't believe..."
- "Doctors HATE this..."
- "This one weird trick..."

### AI-Sounding Hooks
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "More and more people are..."

---

*Hooks should be specific, curiosity-inducing, and backed by real content*
