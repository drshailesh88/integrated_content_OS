## Script Structure Template

### 1. HOOK (0:00 - 0:30)
**Goal**: Stop the scroll, create curiosity gap

**Patterns that work:**
- Surprising statistic: "80% Indians jo yeh karte hain, unhe heart disease ka risk double hai..."
- Myth challenge: "Aapne suna hoga ki [common belief]. Yeh galat hai. Main batata hoon kyun..."
- Story open: "Ek patient aaye mere paas, 42 saal ke. Unka case aapki aankhen khol dega..."
- Direct question: "Kya aap [common thing] karte ho? Yeh aapke dil ke liye kya kar raha hai?"

**Rules:**
- No generic greetings ("Namaste dosto")
- First 5 seconds = most critical
- Create information gap that MUST be filled
- Promise value without clickbait

### 2. INTRO + CREDIBILITY (0:30 - 1:30)
**Goal**: Establish authority, set expectations

**Elements:**
- Quick self-intro (if needed for new viewers)
- Why this topic matters NOW
- What viewer will learn (roadmap)
- Stakes: What's at risk if they don't watch

**Example:**
> "Main Dr. Shailesh, interventional cardiologist. Pichhle 15 saalon mein hazaaron patients dekhe hain jinke paas yeh information hoti toh unka heart attack avoid ho sakta tha. Aaj main aapko exactly woh bataunga jo main apne patients ko clinic mein batata hoon..."

### 3. BODY - Main Content (1:30 - 8:00)
**Goal**: Deliver promised value with engagement

**Structure Options:**

**Option A: Listicle (3-5 points)**
```
Point 1: [Setup → Evidence → Practical takeaway]
Transition: "Ab doosri baat..."
Point 2: [Setup → Evidence → Practical takeaway]
...
```

**Option B: Story-driven**
```
Patient case introduction
What happened (tension)
Medical explanation (education)
Resolution
Lessons learned
```

**Option C: Myth-busting**
```
State the myth clearly
Why people believe it
What evidence actually says
The nuanced truth
What to do instead
```

**Engagement Beats (every 2-3 minutes):**
- Question to viewer: "Aapko kya lagta hai?"
- Surprising reveal: "Lekin yahan twist hai..."
- Relatable moment: "Aap bhi soch rahe honge..."
- Pattern interrupt: Change pace, tone, or visual suggestion

### 4. SUMMARY + CTA (8:00 - 10:00)
**Goal**: Reinforce learning, drive action

**Summary:**
- Recap 3 key points (brief)
- One sentence takeaway
- "Agar sirf ek cheez yaad rakhni ho..."

**CTA (choose one primary):**
- Subscribe: "Is channel pe aisi videos regularly aati hain..."
- Comment: "Apna sawaal neeche likhiye, main jawab dunga..."
- Share: "Kisi apne ko bhejiye jinke kaam aa sake..."
- Watch next: "Yeh topic aur detail mein samjhna ho toh yeh video dekhiye..."

## Hinglish Writing Rules

### Word Choice Matrix

| Context | Use Hindi | Use English |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Emotions | Dil, zindagi, takleef | - |
| Medical terms | - | Cholesterol, BP, diabetes |
| Actions | Samjhiye, dekhiye, sochiye | - |
| Data | - | 80%, studies show |
| Body parts | - | Heart, arteries, blood |
| Severity | Khatarnak, serious | Critical, emergency |

### Sentence Patterns

**Explanation:**
> "Cholesterol do type ka hota hai - LDL jo 'bad cholesterol' hai, aur HDL jo 'good cholesterol' hai. LDL zyada ho toh arteries mein jam jaata hai..."

**Evidence citation:**
> "2023 ki ek study, jisme 50,000 Indians the, usme paya gaya ki..."

**Practical advice:**
> "Toh aap kya karein? Simple hai - daily 30 minute walk, dinner 8 baje se pehle, aur sodium kam..."

### Transitions (Hindi)
- Point to point: "Ab doosri baat...", "Teen number...", "Sabse zaroori baat..."
- Contrast: "Lekin...", "Haan, magar...", "Yahan twist hai..."
- Emphasis: "Dhyan se suniye...", "Yeh important hai...", "Yeh mat bhooliye..."
- Story: "Ek patient ka case batata hoon...", "Mere saath kya hua..."

## Example Script Snippet

**Topic**: Why walking is better than gym for heart health

```
[HOOK - 0:00]
Gym mein ghanton pasina bahane wale log, suniye. Ek 60 saal ki aunty jo sirf roz subah park mein walk karti hain, unka heart aapse zyada healthy ho sakta hai. Shocked? Main batata hoon research kya kehti hai...

[INTRO - 0:25]
Main Dr. Shailesh, interventional cardiologist. Heart patients roz dekhta hoon. Aur sabse common question jo mujhe milta hai - "Doctor, gym jaayein ya walk karein?" Aaj main is confusion ko permanently khatam karta hoon. Evidence ke saath.

[BODY - 0:50]
Pehli baat samjhiye - heart ke liye kaunsi exercise best hai?

Studies kehti hain ki Zone 2 cardio - matlab aisi exercise jisme aap thoda breathless ho, but baat kar sako - yeh heart ke liye GOLD STANDARD hai.

Aur yeh Zone 2, brisk walking se easily mil jaata hai. Gym mein heavy weights uthana? Woh muscle ke liye achha hai, heart ke liye utna nahi.

[Evidence beat]
Duke University ki study - 10,000 logon pe. Jo sirf walk karte the regularly, unka heart disease risk gym jaane walon se SAME tha. Same!

[Engagement beat]
Ab aap soch rahe honge - "Toh gym time waste hai?"

Nahi, bilkul nahi. Gym ke apne fayde hain. Lekin agar SIRF heart health ke liye gym ja rahe ho, toh walk karo aur time bachao...

[TRANSITION]
Doosri baat - kitna walk karna chahiye...
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Starting with "Namaste dosto"** - Boring, skippable
2. **Too much English** - Loses accessibility
3. **No evidence** - Loses credibility
4. **Fear without hope** - Loses trust
5. **Too many points** - Loses attention
6. **Weak ending** - Loses impact
7. **Generic advice** - Loses differentiation
